From the very beginning: Spielbergs life experience

In December 18th, 1946. Spielberg was born in Cincinnati, Ohio in a traditional Jewish family. As years past, his family have been moved to New Jersey because of his father's job, and that is where the beginning of Steven Spielberg entering the cinema for the first time which have left a deep impression on him and have completely changed his life forever. Once Steven has turned 11 years old and moved to Phoenix, Arizona, he grabbed his dad's camera and started making his first home film he created at a young age and it was that moment he became absorbed in the concept of film making.

The young and passionate Steven Spielberg always used the camera to recording family videos and field trips. At that time he have earned a boy scout merit badge of creating a short film called "The last gun" or to what Spielberg like to title it "gun smog."

But this is not the only film he made during his youth. At the age of 13, he have made a short 40 minute war film, titled "Escape to Nowhere." Where his short film have won first place in a statewide competition. However, sometimes things never traverse in the same course because his family often move a lot and always ending up in new neighborhoods and in a new environment. Despite the suffering of being bullied and his struggle of his jewish heritage, every Saturday in Phoenix he always watch the local theaters that gives him the influence and inspiration to continue persuing film making.

Throught his early years as a teenager he continued to create about 28 adventure films until during his high school he went and created his first ever featured film "Firelight" a science fiction adventure film. As the years go by after his graduation, his parents were divorced and he moved to Saratoga, California with his father where he worked as an unpaid assistant in universal studio. He enrolled at the California State University long beach to study english as a backup career if his goal to become a filmmaker did not end well. But, little did he know that Universal gave Spielberg an opprotunity to write and direct a short film for a theatrical film called "Amblin" which have resulted Speilberg in a 7 year directing contract and made him the most youngest directer in a long-term deal with the Hollywood Studio.

The youngest director in Hollywood

An iconic and memorable thriller movie about the Great White Shark. However, for Spielberg, this was the most challenging movie he have directed compared from his time of directing for television mostly due to technical issues of the shark, many delays, and the camera being completely soaked which forced Spielberg to shoot many scenes of the shark being hinted at instead of showing it.
